The Book of Carp

A Collection of Quotes

In the days of streaming there arose one whose voice carried across the currents of the Net: Carp, they were named, and for five years they shone, and a fellowship gathered round their light.

From their words was first wrought a collection of eight hundred sayings, bound into the earliest Book of Carp. But that tome was lost, swallowed by silence.

Yet while the stream endured, a greater gathering began. Utterances were preserved anew, and from them the present Book of Carp was born. When Carp's voice itself fell silent, the Loremaster and a few faithful followers carried the work onward, guarding the memory through the years.

Thus the Book of Carp endures: not the first, but the lasting relic of a vanished stream. So long as its words are spoken, Carp's voice shall never wholly fade.
